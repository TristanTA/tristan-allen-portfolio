# Mira Lens

Offline-first AR and multimodal sensing concept focused on minimal interface overhead and real-time field use.

## Problem

Wearable AI systems can become distracting very quickly. In outdoor settings especially, every overlay, alert, and detection decision competes with situational awareness instead of supporting it.

Mira Lens explores how a lightweight AR assistant could surface useful environmental information without overwhelming the user.

## System Built

Mira Lens is a concept-stage system combining computer vision, audio recognition, and a minimal overlay model for nature-oriented use cases. It is not one of the flagship engineering proofs in the portfolio, but it is valuable for showing how I think about multimodal AI systems under interface and hardware constraints.

## Stack and Architecture

- Vision pipeline for object or scene recognition
- Audio classification workflow for environmental sound signals
- Offline-first inference direction
- Lightweight HUD concept for low-distraction output

## Constraints

- Low-power hardware targets
- Outdoor lighting and noisy environmental conditions
- Very limited interface real estate
- Need to reduce interruption and visual clutter

## What Works Today

- Clear multimodal system framing
- Strong constraint-driven product concept
- Useful architecture thinking around offline inference and low-distraction output

## Evidence and Current Status

Mira Lens is best understood as a supporting concept project. It shows system thinking and product constraints more than it shows mature implementation depth, which is why it no longer leads the homepage over stronger AI/ML engineering projects.

## Why It Matters

Even though Mira Lens is not a primary hiring signal for this portfolio, it still reflects an important part of how I build: I care about whether an AI system can fit a real context of use instead of becoming a technically interesting distraction.

## Next Engineering Milestone

- Prototype more of the multimodal inference path
- Test output timing and interface density choices
- Clarify which use cases justify the additional hardware complexity
